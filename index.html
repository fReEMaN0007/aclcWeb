Gov't scholarships based on academic standing

CNN Philippines Staff
February 20, 2019

The Department of Education (DepEd) reminded Wednesday the bottomline of awarding government scholarships is academic standing and not a student's engagement in other activities.
"Hindi tinatanggalan mo ng scholarship dahil engaged in other activities. Even in high school and elementary ine-encourage natin 'yung notion ng the 'whole child or the whole learner, whole personality.' Pati pag-identify ng honor students kasama 'yung extracurricular activities so bottomline, sa scholarships of course is academic standings (ang basis)," Briones said in a media briefing.

No Classes in Taytay

March 16, 2020

Section 5. Classes in all levels, both public and private are hereby suspended beginning March 20, 2020 until April 14, 2020, unless the suspension of classes is lifted if circumstances so warrant, subject, however, to the conditions imposed under paragraph B.1 of Resolution No. 11 s. 2020 of the Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (IATF-MEID), which is, that all students shall continue to fulfill their educational requirements and that they shall remain in their homes during the period of suspension of classes.
